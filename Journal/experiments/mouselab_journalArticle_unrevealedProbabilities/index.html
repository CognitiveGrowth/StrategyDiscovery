Consent to Participate in Research

Betting Game

This HIT is a psychology experiment being conducted by Paul Krueger from Professor Tom Griffiths's Computational Cognitive Science Lab at the University of California, Berkeley. In order to consent to participate, you MUST meet the following criteria:
  • 18 years of age or older.
  • Fluent speaker of English.
  • Have not participated in this experiment before.
This study is designed to look at how people make decisions. In this task, you will be asked to make choices and answer questions related to those decisions.

The study will take about 20 minutes, and will pay $0.25 plus a performance-dependent bonus of up to a maximum of $9.99. The performance bonus is explained in more detail in the instructions that follow.

Your participation in this research is voluntary. You may refrain from answering any questions that make you uncomfortable and may withdraw your participation at any time without penalty by returning the HIT on MTurk and emailing the experimenter at the address provided below.

Other than monetary compensation, participating will provide no direct benefits to you. But, we hope that this research will benefit society at large. Many problems that people can solve very easily are very difficult to automate (to have computers solve). By revealing more about how humans learn and think, our research may help others to build and program computers that can solve these kinds of problems.

Your MTurk worker ID may be connected to individual responses, but we will not be asking for any additional personally identifying information, and we will handle responses as confidentially as possible. We cannot however guarantee the confidentiality of information transmitted over the Internet. We will be keeping data collected as part of this experiment indefinitely.

If you have any questions about the study, feel free to contact Paul Krueger. He can be reached at paul.m.krueger@berkeley.edu. If you have concerns about your rights as a participant in this experiment, please contact UC Berkeley's Committee for Protection of Human Subjects at (510) 642-7461 or subjects@berkeley.edu.

Instructions

Welcome! In this HIT you will play a series of 20 betting games. In each game, you will choose one of seven bets. After you have placed your bet, a ball will be drawn from a lottery machine with 100 balls. In the example below, the bin contains 16 yellow balls, 33 brown balls, 18 blue balls, and 33 purple balls. Which kind of ball wins you the most money depends on the bet you chose.

The value of each color ball is determined by which of the seven bets you make. Before making a bet, you may observe the value of any type of ball by clicking on the grey box corresponding to it. The value hidden behind each grey box is unique. You may observe as many or as few values as you wish. This may help you decide which bet to make. However, there is a small price to pay for each observation: each observation costs $0.01.

For example, by clicking on the first box under Bet 6, it is revealed that the 16 yellow balls are each worth $6.96. (But the value of the yellow balls for other bets is different).

Clicking on the third box under Bet 2 reveals that Bet 2 wins $5.86 if a blue ball is drawn....

Bet 2 wins $1.98 if a purple ball is drawn...

And Bet 6 wins $2.01 if a purple ball is drawn. You may also wish to explore other values. Note that you have paid $0.04 for making four observations.

If you spend less than 25 seconds making observations, then you will have to wait for the remaining seconds before you can make a bet. For instance, if you spend only 5 seconds collecting information, then you will have to wait 20 seconds.

When you have finished revealing grey boxes, you will select a Bet by clicking on a red box. In this pretend example, let's imagine that you select Bet 3. You would do this by clicking on "Bet 3"...

The outcome was a purple ball, and you would have won $5.49, as indicated by the feedback. For the sake of curiosity, let's see the values that would have been revealed if you had clicked on all of the grey boxes...

Note that you would pay $0.28 for observing all 28 boxes.

After making a bet, you move-on to a new game. With each new game, the value behind each grey box will change. Also, the number of yellow, brown, blue, and purple balls will change, and therefore the most likely outcome may change, too.

You will win real money for your bets: You will play 20 seperate games, and therefore you will win 20 seperate rewards. At the end of the task, one of these 20 rewards will be randomly selected, and paid to you as real bonus money.
